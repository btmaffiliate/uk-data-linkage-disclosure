## PART 1 — Who to go to (in priority order)

### Tier 1 — built for exactly this
1. **Foxglove** — `foxglove.org.uk` — UK legal non-profit that *litigates* government data/tech. Track
   record: NHS Data Store / Palantir, DWP algorithm challenges, care.data-style fights. **Your #1.** Send
   the master report + the GDPR Legal Exposure doc. Angle: **NHS↔justice linkage, function creep, missing
   DPIA.**
2. **noyb (None of Your Business)** — `noyb.eu` — Max Schrems' NGO; files strategic GDPR complaints
   professionally across the EU. **Best for any EU deployment** (European Medicines Agency, Germany/Destatis).
   This is literally their operating model.
3. **AWO** — `awo.agency` — boutique **data-rights law firm** (if you want a solicitor directly rather than
   an NGO). They litigate/advise on GDPR and act for journalists and NGOs.

### Tier 2 — campaigners & specialists
4. **medConfidential** — `medconfidential.org` — NHS/health-data-linkage specialists. Perfect for the **NHS
   England Person_ID / NHS↔justice** angle.
5. **Open Rights Group** — `openrightsgroup.org` — UK digital rights; campaigns + some legal.
6. **Big Brother Watch** — `bigbrotherwatch.org.uk` — surveillance campaigns; very media-effective.
7. **Privacy International** — `privacyinternational.org` — strategic litigation, international; they wrote
   the Aadhaar analysis.

### Tier 3 — international / specific verticals
8. **EDRi (European Digital Rights)** — `edri.org` — EU-wide umbrella; good for the cross-EU framing.
9. **Internet Freedom Foundation** — `internetfreedom.in` — India / Aadhaar.

### Journalists
- **The Bureau of Investigative Journalism** — non-profit, built for this.
- **The Guardian** data/tech desk; **Computer Weekly** (broke major NHS-data stories).

### The regulator
- **ICO** — `ico.org.uk` — for the formal complaint (Part 2). EU equivalents: the relevant national DPA, or
  the **EDPS** for EU bodies like the EMA.

### How to approach each (the pitch)
Two short paragraphs. Real name. Report + GDPR doc attached. Sober:
> *"I've compiled a public-interest analysis, entirely from published documents, of cross-domain citizen
> record linkage and its data-protection exposure. I believe there's a credible transparency / purpose-
> limitation / special-category-data issue with [specific deployment]. The report and a GDPR exposure note
> are attached. Would this be of interest?"*
No "leak," no "war." Offer them the work; let them carry it.

## PART 2 — ICO complaint skeleton (for a solicitor to sharpen)

**Complainant:** [your name / contact] — **Date:** [ ]

**Controller complained of:** [the specific body — e.g. NHS England / ONS / Ministry of Justice]

**Processing complained of:** [the specific deployment — e.g. cross-domain linkage of NHS patient records
with justice/administrative data via Person_ID, as described in the controller's own published material]

**Grounds (plead narrowly — do NOT plead "no consent / no opt-out" as the lead):**
1. **Art 5(1)(a) — fairness & transparency.** Data subjects are not informed their records are linked
   across domains; the processing is not transparent.
2. **Art 5(1)(b) — purpose limitation.** Data obtained/linked for statistical purposes is used for
   operational identification of individuals (beyond the Art 89 statistics cover), without a separate valid
   basis.
3. **Art 9 & Art 10 — special-category & criminal data.** Linking health data with justice/criminal data
   engages heightened conditions; the complaint asks the ICO to verify each is satisfied for every linkage.
4. **Art 35 — DPIA.** Request disclosure of the Data Protection Impact Assessment(s); assess whether one
   exists, is adequate, and addressed these risks (and whether prior consultation under Art 36 was needed).
5. **Proportionality** (Art 5(1)(c) minimisation + Charter/ECHR Art 8): population-scale, indefinite,
   cross-domain linkage may exceed what is necessary and proportionate.

**What I ask the ICO to do:**
- Investigate the above; **require the controller to produce the DPIA**; assess the lawful basis for the
  special-category/criminal-data linkage; assess transparency compliance; and consider enforcement.

**Evidence relied on:** the controller's own published transparency records / methodology pages, plus the
attached report (all public-source).

**Note:** *Not legal advice. A data-protection solicitor should settle the grounds and check every article
against current UK GDPR text and ICO guidance before filing. FOI / subject-access requests can surface
whether a DPIA exists and is adequate — do that first.*

---

## The sequence
1. **FOI the DPIA** for the chosen deployment (cheap, fast, surfaces Door 4).
2. **Send the two-paragraph pitch to Foxglove and/or AWO** with the report + GDPR doc.
3. If they bite, **let them lead.** If you go direct, **file the narrow ICO complaint** above.
4. **In parallel**, offer the story to one journalist (BIJ / Guardian) — public pressure + legal pressure
   together is what actually moves a regulator.
5. **Stay surgical and stay open** — one deployment, named grounds, real name, public-source. That's the
   version that wins.
