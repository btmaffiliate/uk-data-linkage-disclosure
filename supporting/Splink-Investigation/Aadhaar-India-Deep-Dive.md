# Aadhaar — The Linked Citizen at Maximum Scale
### India deep-dive: the world's largest biometric ID, the law that shaped it, and the people it has failed

*Public-interest research from public sources. Compiled 6 June 2026. Confidence flagged. Where harms are
documented by campaigns or press rather than official admission, that is stated. Not a legal opinion.*

> **Why Aadhaar matters to the wider story:** it is the consent-free linked state taken to its limit —
> ~1.4 billion people on one biometric key, seeded across welfare, tax and banking. It is where the
> abstract risk becomes concrete: **real exclusion, real deaths, real surveillance architecture** — *and*
> a real court that pushed back. It is both the warning and, partly, the model for how to rein it in.

---

## 1. What it is, and the scale *(CONFIRMED)*
- **Aadhaar** is a **12-digit unique ID** issued by **UIDAI** (Unique Identification Authority of India), a
  statutory body under the Ministry of Electronics & IT.
- **~1.43 billion** Aadhaar numbers generated (≈142.76 crore as of Sept 2025); **>99.9% of adults** — the
  **largest biometric ID system on Earth.**
- **Biometrics held:** a photograph, **ten fingerprints, two iris scans**, plus face authentication (added
  2018) — stored in a centralised **Central Identities Data Repository (CIDR).**
*(Sources: UIDAI; Wikipedia/Aadhaar.)*

## 2. What gets linked — the architecture *(CONFIRMED core; profiling ASSERTED)*
Aadhaar is designed as a **universal linking key**: the number is "seeded" into other databases so records
across services resolve to one person — subsidies, **PAN (income tax)**, bank accounts, SIM cards, pensions,
school enrolment, and more.
- **State Resident Data Hubs (SRDH):** states built resident databases using Aadhaar as the linking key,
  enabling what critics describe as **"360-degree profiles."** *(Reported/ASSERTED — sourced to security
  researchers and advocacy, not official confirmation.)*
- The central design intent: **one identity, queryable across domains** — which is exactly the
  cross-domain linkage at the heart of this whole investigation, but **person-facing and operational**,
  not just statistical.

## 3. The legal basis & the Supreme Court reckoning *(CONFIRMED)*
- **Aadhaar Act 2016** ("Targeted Delivery of Financial and Other Subsidies, Benefits and Services") is the
  statutory basis. It was **passed as a Money Bill** — bypassing the upper house — which became a central
  controversy.
- **Puttaswamy I (2017):** a 9-judge bench held **privacy is a fundamental right** under Article 21 — the
  foundation for everything after.
- **Puttaswamy II / the Aadhaar judgment (26 Sept 2018):** a 5-judge bench, **4:1**, **upheld Aadhaar's core
  constitutionality but read it down:**
  - **Section 57 struck** — private companies can **no longer compel** Aadhaar (the court warned it enabled
    "commercial exploitation … without consent" and **profiling**).
  - **Mandatory bank-account linking (PMLA Rule 9) and SIM/mobile linking struck** — failed the
    proportionality test, no legislative backing.
  - **Aadhaar–PAN (tax) linking upheld**, and **Section 7** (Aadhaar may be required for subsidies/benefits)
    upheld.
  - **Justice D.Y. Chandrachud's dissent** called the Money-Bill route a "fraud on the Constitution" and
    warned explicitly of the surveillance risk — now one of the most-cited privacy dissents in the world.
*(Sources: Supreme Court Observer; Privacy International; SFLC.in.)*

## 4. The human cost — exclusion *(DOCUMENTED by campaign & press; flagged as such)*
This is the part that turns the abstraction into bodies. When biometric authentication **fails** — a worn
fingerprint, no connectivity, a seeding error — eligible people are **denied food rations and entitlements.**
- The **Right to Food Campaign** has documented **~57 starvation deaths across nine states since 2015, at
  least 19 directly linked to Aadhaar** authentication/denial. *(Reported by the campaign and Indian press —
  EPW, Scroll — not official government figures.)*
- Named cases widely reported: **Santoshi Kumari (11, Jharkhand, 2017)** — died after the family's ration
  card wasn't linked to Aadhaar; **Lukhi Murmu (30, Pakur)** — denied rations after the point-of-sale
  machine failed to authenticate her biometrics; **Ruplal Marandi (60, Deoghar, 2017)** — family denied
  rations two months after authentication failed.
- The pattern: officials and ration shops **demanded biometric authentication even where it meant denying
  food to eligible people.** *(Sources: Scroll.in; Economic & Political Weekly; Right to Food Campaign.)*
**This is the linked, consent-free state failing the most vulnerable — not in theory, in practice.**

## 5. Breach & surveillance concerns *(reported)*
- **January 2018 — The Tribune investigation:** reporters obtained login credentials being sold for **₹500**
  granting access to demographic details of Aadhaar holders in ~10 minutes. UIDAI **initially denied** it
  and **filed a complaint against the journalist** rather than fixing it first — itself a scandal.
- The WEF Global Risks Report / Avast characterised Aadhaar-related exposure as among the **largest data
  breaches** globally.
- **Surveillance concern:** because Aadhaar can resolve a person across service domains, critics warn it
  enables **tracking across domains** and the substrate for an "oppressive surveillance state." *(ASSERTED —
  capability/critique, sourced to researchers and advocacy; not a finding of current mass operational
  surveillance.)*

## 6. The fair counter-case *(must be stated)*
- **Real benefits:** Aadhaar **de-duplicated welfare rolls** (removing ghost/fake beneficiaries), enabled
  **direct benefit transfers** that cut leakage, and drove **financial inclusion** (bank accounts, mobile
  money) for hundreds of millions. The government cites large fiscal savings.
- **The court worked:** Puttaswamy is a genuine example of a constitutional democracy **constraining** the
  linked state — striking private and mandatory uses, affirming privacy as fundamental.
- **The harm is partly implementation, not only design:** much exclusion stems from **demanding biometric
  auth where the law didn't require it**, poor connectivity, and seeding errors — fixable failures, though
  that is cold comfort to the families.

---

## 7. The honest bottom line on India
1. **Scale unmatched:** ~1.4 billion people, one biometric key, seeded across the state.
2. **Consent-free by design**, on a statutory basis — the now-familiar pattern, but **operational and
   person-facing**, not merely statistical.
3. **The harm is real and documented:** exclusion deaths (reported, not officially admitted), a serious
   breach, denied entitlements — the clearest present-tense cost in this entire survey.
4. **But it is also the case for hope:** India's Supreme Court **pushed back hard** — proof the linked state
   *can* be constrained when there's a privacy right and a court willing to enforce it.
5. **The lesson for the wider story:** Aadhaar is what the consent-free linked citizen looks like at full
   scale and full stakes — *and* what democratic resistance to it looks like. Both halves are true; tell both.
