**One distinction worth holding onto, because it makes the questions land:**

- **Splink** is the **Ministry of Justice's** own open-source record-linkage tool. It is what drives the "probation-to-police" pilot (and JustLink, and the real-time "Core Person Record"). MoJ questions.
- **Palantir** is a **separate, US-owned commercial platform** (the FDP / the Home Office trial you already asked about). Home Office / DHSC questions.

They are *not* the same system and there is no documented technical link between them — I'm careful never to claim otherwise. They are two layers of the same "joining-up" of citizen data, in different departments. Keeping them apart is what stops the Government deflecting a Splink question by answering about Palantir, or vice versa.

Everything here is built from public documents — no non-public system was accessed — and every claim is graded by how well it's evidenced. Where something is a *pilot* rather than a national rollout, or where the legal basis is implied rather than stated, I've flagged it as such; I'd rather under-claim than hand the department an easy rebuttal.
