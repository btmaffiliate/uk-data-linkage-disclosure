# The Questions That Matter
## A one-page spine for parliamentary and City Hall scrutiny

**Prepared for:** the Office of Iqbal Mohamed MP (Dewsbury and Batley) — by Brandon Myers, brandonmyers.net — June 2026

These questions are built to be **unbreakable**: each is anchored to a confirmed fact or phrased conditionally, so there is no premise to dispute, and none can be deflected with *"we do not hold that information"* — because they ask **why**, and a refusal to explain *why* is itself the story.

---

### The framing question

> **Why — across both the Metropolitan Police and the Ministry of Justice — is the most sensitive citizen and officer data being joined up on these platforms with the oversight switched off: under-threshold contracts, no published Data Protection Impact Assessments, no ethical reviews, no opt-outs — and who decided that was acceptable?**

---

### Metropolitan Police — Palantir "Unified Data Platform"

1. **How many** contracts did the MPS enter into with Palantir for the Unified Data Platform, **what was the value of each**, and **what is the combined total including any extension**?
2. If the programme was delivered under **more than one contract**, **why were they not aggregated** for the £500,000 threshold at which Deputy Mayor approval and a published decision are required (MOPAC Scheme of Delegation and Consent, para 4.8; Procurement Act 2023, Schedule 3)?
3. **Why** was a Palantir pilot processing officers' sickness, absence, overtime and **work-device geolocation** data not put to the Deputy Mayor for approval, with a published Policing and Crime Decision?
4. **Why** is the extension cost being withheld as *"commercially sensitive"* — what is that protecting?
5. **Why** was there no published DPIA or ethical review before profiling officers' special-category and location data?
6. **Why** Palantir, sole-sourced — when that **same single-supplier failing** led the Deputy Mayor to **block** the separate ~£50m Unified Operational Analytics deal weeks later?

### Ministry of Justice — Splink

7. **Why** is **ethnicity** processed in the Splink linkage with **no Article 9 condition named** in any public document?
8. **Why** has **no DPIA been published**, and **why** is there **no notification or opt-out** for the people whose probation records are sent to the police **daily** under the North Essex pilot?
9. **Why** does the "Data First" record say the data is *"not for operational decision-making"* while the "Splink Master Record" documents exactly that — operational, person-identifying use?
